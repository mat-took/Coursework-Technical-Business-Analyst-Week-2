# JTBD Statements

## Customer

"When I call, I want to be financially prepared so that I am not surprised by the case." - MEDIUM

## Agent

"When I join a case, I want a seamless handover so that I can resolve it quickly." - MEDIUM

"When looking at cases, I want to be looking at data that makes sense, so that I
am not confused." - LOW

## Operations Manager

"When Im looking at data, I want consistency across sources so that I can work efficiently." - HIGH

- Consistent data ensures all business functions using it are more effective, and ensures business
	integrity when it comes to sending information to regulatory bodies.
- Evidence includes time spent on case, time between handover and starting case, metric related to how easy it is to get
	in contact with delinquent accounts.
- Will affect phase 1 scope by defining relevant stakeholders in mapping process.


"When adopting a new workflow, I want to know it will work immediately so that my
team can do their job." - HIGH

- In terms of business impact, this determines how effective the project is in achieving its goal. If this step is not done
	well, then the project may be more of a burden than a boon.
- Detailed process maps (swim lane, as is, to be) with pain points will enable this job.
- This will determine exactly who we talk to, and whose work we have to consider in phase 1.

## Finance Partner

"When building a case for smart-recovery, I want data and assumptions so that leadership
trusts it." - HIGH
	
- The project will not be implemented without this, making its business impact great.
- This JTBD will require a range of evidence, most of which will be financial from ROI models.
- Phase 1 scope will be loosely defined by the extent to which we go to procure evidence for
	this job. Where we collect evidence from will map out the outer boundaries of this
	project's influence.
